## The two frames, and why they matter here

**Energy** and **compression** are the two lenses under everything the project does — and each handed back one concrete, high-value idea:

1. **Energy (t5.1): the closed-form namer can become analog-native.** A ridge/Gram solve is the *minimum of a quadratic energy*, which is exactly the equilibrium an analog oscillator/RLC network settles into (`⟨x⟩ = A⁻¹b`, covariance `A⁻¹` — and Melanson 2025 *built* such a chip). So the namer's re-fit is not stuck as a numpy `solve`; it has a **physical-settle realization** — the single strongest lever for the queued analog-realism pass. Under it all sits **Landauer**: the cost is *erasing*, so a resident-weight, settle-don't-erase substrate is on the cheap side of physics. And **Hoover's Energy Transformer** is the north-star think-loop already published (depth = settle-steps; the energy value = the halt-feeling).

2. **Compression (t5.2): the spare-capacity hypothesis is superposition + lottery ticket — and it has never been measured.** The substrate's committed sparsity is the *precondition* superposition needs; the capacity bet and the substrate property are one claim. P11 half-confirmed it with retention numbers, but the project has never reported a **codelength** (bits) or counted the **used feature dictionary** (a sparse autoencoder on the frozen bulk). Both are cheap and would turn a hypothesis into a measurement. Honest counterweight: forward-only lacks grokking's weight-norm *cleanup* pressure, so the slack could be dead memorization — testable, not assumed.

## Carry-back, in one line each

- **Design equation for the realism pass:** *namer re-fit = physical relaxation of a quadratic energy* (t5.1).
- **Two numbers the project is missing:** *prequential codelength + SAE feature-dictionary size* (t5.2).
- **A mechanism for the P11 floor:** *superposition degrades under temporal correlation* (t5.2 → ties t1.7, t2.5).
