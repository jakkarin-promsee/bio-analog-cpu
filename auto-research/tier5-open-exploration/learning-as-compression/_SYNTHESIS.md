# Synthesis — Learning IS Compression  (Tier 5)
**The question:** What is the information-theoretic account of *why a model fits on-chip* — MDL (learning = compression), intrinsic dimension, double descent, the Lottery Ticket, and superposition/polysemanticity — and what does it say about our **spare-capacity** hypothesis?

**Already in `draft6.0/research/`:** the curated dossier `north-star/13-compression.md` covers the anchors — **MDL** (Rissanen 1978; Hinton & van Camp 1993), **double descent** (Belkin 2019), **intrinsic dimension** (Li 2018), **Lottery Ticket** (Frankle 2019), and **Toy Models of Superposition** (Elhage 2022) — and already names our spare-capacity hypothesis as superposition + lottery ticket. This sweep **extends** it into the 2023–2026 threads the dossier only points at: superposition↔double-descent↔memorization (Henighan 2023), superposition-at-scale via sparse dictionaries (Templeton 2024), grokking-as-compression (Power 2022; Nanda 2023), operational MDL (Blier & Ollivier 2018), deep double descent (Nakkiran 2019), and the compression→convergence link (Platonic, Huh 2024).

## The landscape
There is one idea under all of it: **a model that generalizes is a model that compressed its data** (MDL). The field has three moves that turn this from slogan into engineering. (1) **Measure the compression** — Blier & Ollivier make MDL operational with the *prequential (online) code*: the streaming predictive loss, in bits, *is* the description length, and deep nets compress losslessly even after paying for parameters. (2) **Explain why over-capacity is safe** — double descent (Belkin, then Nakkiran's deep/epoch/sample version) shows test error peaks at the interpolation threshold and *descends* past it, because SGD's implicit bias picks the smoothest/lowest-norm — most compressible — interpolant; the incompressible noise cancels. (3) **Locate where the capacity goes** — the Lottery Ticket says 80–90% of weights are overhead-for-search, not representation; **superposition** (Elhage) says the *used* capacity holds *more features than neurons* as near-orthogonal directions, enabled by sparsity.

The 2023–2026 frontier fuses these. **Henighan 2023** shows superposition stores *either datapoints or features* as directions, and double descent is the switch between the two — memorization and generalization are one mechanism on different objects. **Templeton 2024** proves superposition is real in a production model by pulling millions of monosemantic features out with a sparse autoencoder (dictionary learning) — the *measurement instrument* for "how many features does a representation hold?" **Grokking** (Power 2022) is compression caught in slow motion — generalization arriving long after the loss flattens — and **Nanda 2023** dissects it into *memorize → form a compact circuit → clean up the memorized scaffold*, with **progress measures** that see the compression the loss hides. **Huh 2024 (Platonic)** closes the loop: because competent models are pushed by a compression/simplicity bias toward the *simplest* representation of a shared reality, independent models **converge** to the same geometry.

## How WE differ  ← the money section
Our object is the **compression theory made into a substrate**. The spare-capacity hypothesis the author intuited — "each neuron is under-allocated; the slack is shareable across tasks" — is not a guess: it is **superposition** (slack filled by near-orthogonal features under sparsity) plus the **Lottery Ticket** (most capacity is search-overhead, not representation). Our committed **sparsity** is precisely the precondition superposition needs, so our substrate property and our capacity bet are the same claim from two sides. And our streaming evaluation already computes the **prequential code** in all but name — P11's prequential accuracy is one `−log p` away from reporting our object's *description length in bits*, which is the on-chip-fit argument in its native currency.

But three honest gaps separate us from the theory. First, **we have never measured our compression.** We claim the bulk holds many tasks in bounded capacity, but we have no dictionary-size count (Templeton) and no codelength (Blier). P11 *half*-confirmed the hypothesis with retention numbers; it did not measure superposition. Second, **our engine lacks a cleanup pressure.** Grokking/Nanda's compression completes because weight decay *removes* the memorized scaffold; double descent's benefit comes from an *implicit low-norm bias*. Our forward-only local rule has no explicit global norm pressure — so the mechanism that turns memorized directions into shareable slack may be missing, and the slack could be dead memorization rather than reusable capacity (the pessimistic reading). Third, **the Lottery Ticket's init-lock threatens "shareable across tasks":** winning tickets are tied to a specific initialization found by search, which is not obviously reusable for future tasks. Our sharing bet needs the slack to be task-general — which is exactly what the Platonic convergence result would grant *at scale*, and exactly what our tiny substrate may be too small to reach.

## The gap / what we haven't tried
- **Report the codelength.** Add prequential *bits* (not just accuracy) to our streaming metrics (Blier). "Compression per task" becomes a retention measure and the direct on-chip-fit number.
- **Count the dictionary.** Train a small **sparse autoencoder** (closed-form / random-projection + L1, no backprop) on the frozen bulk's activations and count interpretable atoms (Templeton). That number is the operational measure of *spare capacity used* — the missing measurement behind P11's half-confirmation.
- **A compression progress-measure as a convergence invariant.** Grokking shows flat loss hides ongoing (or stalled) compression; replace/augment "loss-slope" with a probe/restricted-tap "structure-formed" measure (Nanda) so we know when the bulk has *organized* a task, not just stopped moving.
- **Storage-regime probe (memorize vs generalize).** On a task the bulk WINS, test whether hidden directions align with *features* or *individual samples* (Henighan) — this predicts which side of double descent we live on, and when a tight-capacity stream (C=20) will FLOOR.
- **Kernel-alignment generality probe.** Measure whether the frozen bulk's relational geometry aligns with a strong reference encoder (Huh) — positive evidence that the slack is *genuinely shareable* across future tasks.

## Leads spawned
- **Operational compression metrics for the substrate** — prequential codelength + sparse-autoencoder dictionary size as first-class reported numbers (highest-value, directly closes P11's half-confirmation).
- **Compression progress-measures as a convergence invariant** — restricted/excluded-loss-style probes for "structure formed."
- **Implicit-regularization / weight-norm dynamics** — does our forward-only substrate have a cleanup/low-norm pressure, or must sleep supply it? (grokking's missing-engine question)
- **Compression-generalization bounds** (Arora et al. compression bounds; intrinsic dimension of images, Pope 2021) — the "task is tiny → fits on-chip" thread, quantified.
- **Superposition under temporal correlation** — the mechanism behind our P11 autocorrelated-stream FLOOR (interference when features co-activate).
