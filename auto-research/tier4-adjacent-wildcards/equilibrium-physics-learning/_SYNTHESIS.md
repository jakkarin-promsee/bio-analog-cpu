# Synthesis — Equilibrium Propagation & physics-based learning  (Tier 4)
**The question:** Is physics-based learning (EqProp / coupled learning / physical learning) a **threat** to our forward-only choice, a **complement**, or the **learning rule of the north-star recurrent loop** — given that the settling loop will reach equilibrium anyway?
**Already in `draft6.0/research/`:** EqProp is a named prior we passed over (forward-only SCFF chosen for the bulk). The Laydevant Ising-EqProp paper is already carded from the loop side ([tier3 / t3.2](../../tier3-north-star/recurrent-thinking/laydevant-2024-ising-eqprop.md)); this topic re-cards it from the physics-annealer angle and adds the full contrastive-learning family it belongs to. Nothing else in this family is in the curated library.

## The landscape (3 paragraphs)

**One skeleton, seven+ dialects.** Every algorithm here shares a single shape: let a physical system **settle** to equilibrium (an energy or dissipated-power minimum — *settling is the forward pass*), measure a local quantity at each element, produce a second state, and update each parameter by the **difference** of the two measurements. The family fans out only in how the second state is made: hard-clamp Contrastive Learning, one-sided EqProp (±β), **centered EP** (the symmetric ±β nudge that cancels bias to second order — Scellier 2023's outright winner), and coupled learning (clamp toward a convex mix of free output and target — the Penn resistor-network variant). Laborieux 2022's holomorphic trick collapses the two static phases into **one** by driving the nudge as a complex-plane oscillation and demodulating the exact gradient locally — a lock-in amplifier per synapse, ImageNet-32 at backprop parity. The decisive shared property: because both states are measured on the **same imperfect hardware**, systematic device error (nonlinearity, drift, variation) appears in both terms and **cancels in the difference** — this is why the family survives real analog where backprop-on-analog fails (it differentiates an idealized model the device doesn't match).

**The same rule, realized in every substrate.** Yi/Kendall 2022 runs it on tantalum-oxide memristor crossbars (MADEM), classifying Braille in-array with a projected >10⁴× energy edge. Laydevant 2024 runs it on a **D-Wave quantum annealer**: free phase = forward anneal to ground state (the inference), nudge phase = **reverse anneal** (re-open tunneling, bias the outputs toward target), update = spin-spin correlation difference per coupler — 88.8% on MNIST/100, and the initially stochastic annealer becomes deterministic on inference as training sharpens the landscape. Dillavou 2024 (CLLN / coupled learning) does it with **no processor anywhere** — a self-adjusting transistor mesh where Kirchhoff's laws settle the forward pass and each edge nudges its own gate charge from its own free-vs-clamped voltage difference; learns XOR in seconds, infers in microseconds, at picojoules/transistor, and exhibits spectral bias like an ANN. Stern & Murugan 2023 is the field map ("physical learning") unifying flow networks, elastic materials, and self-assembly under model-free local-rule adaptation.

**Two honest, universal costs.** (1) **Supervised and settling** — every member needs a *target* to make the second state, and needs equilibrium reached at least twice per example (centered-EP: two extra settles; holomorphic: one settle + oscillation-period averaging; Ising: two anneals + a digital transverse-field conductor). (2) **Scale is unproven at the physics level** — Stern's review is blunt that no physical learner exceeds toy scale; holomorphic EqProp (ImageNet-32) is the family's ceiling and it lives in simulation, not fabricated matter. The field has no label-free member at scale (CLLNs are only now turning unsupervised — the Restricted Kirchhoff Machine, on the leads list).

## How WE differ  ← the money section

**Verdict: NOT a threat to the bulk; it is the recurrent loop's learning rule — a complement via merge.**

The clean placement: **EqProp and SCFF solve different problems, and the north star has room for both.** SCFF is our **feed-forward, label-free, one-pass** organizer — it grows a representation from an unlabeled stream. Every EqProp-family member here is **supervised** (the second state needs a target) and **settling** (needs equilibrium ≥2×). So EqProp cannot replace the bulk without deleting the two properties the bulk exists to provide (label-free + one-pass). It is therefore **not a threat to what we committed to.**

It **is** a threat to exactly one narrow claim — "forward-only is the best analog-native *static-accuracy* learning rule" — and we already concede that crown (P4/P10). Scellier 2023 holds it convincingly: centered EP out-trains anything forward-only on deep conv Hopfield nets. If a reviewer asks "why not EqProp," the honest answer is *not* "it's worse" — it's "**it solves supervised static training; our bulk solves label-free continual organization**," and this synthesis is both the threat and the disarming of it.

**The merge is the real find, and it is architecturally clean.** The north-star loop already plans to **settle** (monDEQ-style contractive, tier3/t3.2). Laydevant shows that the *same settle*, re-run with a small output bias, yields a valid weight update from purely local correlation differences — the loop's compute and the loop's learning are the **same physics**. So the committed shape is:

> **SCFF stays the forward-only label-free bulk; EqProp becomes the learning rule of the settling recurrent loop** — the loop reads (free settle) and learns (nudged re-settle) in one substrate, giving the loop weights something the closed-form namer structurally cannot: **depth of credit into the recurrent weights**. The namer gets its direction from a zero-settle closed-form solve; EqProp-in-the-loop must earn its cost by buying credit-assignment the namer can't touch.

**Honest about EqProp's costs inside our economy.** The merge is not free: (1) a **target must exist** — so EqProp fires only where the labeled trickle reaches the loop, never on the unlabeled bulk stream; (2) it needs **a settle + a nudged second phase**, which is a real tax — though this family has been shrinking it: holomorphic EqProp removes the *phase-sequencing controller* entirely (one settle + AC lock-in, no stored first equilibrium), and centered-EP is already the benchmarked ±β variant (do not re-derive). (3) The two-phase choreography wants **an external digital conductor** (the transverse-field schedule in Laydevant is not free). The economy-legal move — and the one that prices EqProp *into* our 80/20 gate instead of against it — is a **gate-fired nudged re-settle: run the second phase only when the drift gate trips.** That turns EqProp from an always-on tax into a metered, occasional loop-consolidation step, the same shape as our sleep.

**What's genuinely ours vs re-invention:** the *bulk* (forward-only + label-free) is genuinely outside this family — no member is both. The *substrate story* (resident analog weights updated by a locally measured difference, error-cancellation-by-differencing) is **independent convergence**: Yi/Dillavou/Stern tell our own value proposition (model-free device-error tolerance, native continual re-adaptation) in fabricated hardware and condensed-matter language. That is validation, not threat — but it also denies us any claim to have *invented* local physical learning.

## The gap / what we haven't tried

- **The biggest untested lever: does the SCFF contrastive-pair difference *also* self-cancel systematic device error?** Yi 2022's whole robustness argument is that differencing two states measured on the same imperfect devices cancels their shared error. Our SCFF update is *also* a contrastive pair difference — a dedicated sim probe (inject systematic Scap nonlinearity/asymmetry, measure whether the SCFF delta cancels it the way MADEM's does) would tell us whether the deferred PVT-realism pass will be **kinder** to SCFF than to any rule that trusts a device model. This is the single highest-value experiment this topic surfaces.
- **Sim an EqProp rung on the loop and price it against the closed-form namer** — centered-EP (two extra settles) vs holomorphic (one settle + AC averaging) as the cheaper physical schedule; success bar = the loop weights gain credit-depth the closed-form namer cannot, at a settle cost the drift-gate can afford.
- **Gate-fired nudged re-settle**: wire the loop's second (nudged) phase to the existing drift-gate trigger; measure fires-per-example and accuracy-per-settle against always-on EqProp.
- **Two-terminal-local audit of SCFF** (Dillavou's realism bar): what exactly must each Scap cell *measure* per update, and is it strictly two-terminal like a CLLN edge? A ready-made frame for the realism pass.
- **Import the fast-settle apparatus** (Scellier's async energy-minimization + 16-bit, 13.5× faster) as the simulator machinery for any EqProp rung — don't rebuild it.

## Leads spawned
- **Restricted Kirchhoff Machine** (arXiv:2509.15842, PNAS 2026) — CLLNs go *label-free* (RBM-like circuit): directly our regime, the strongest future card in this family.
- **Kendall et al. 2020 — end-to-end analog EqProp** (arXiv:2006.01981) — the crossbar-EqProp seminal theory both Yi and Scellier build on.
- **Laborieux & Zenke 2023 — Jacobian homeostasis** (arXiv:2309.02214) — attacks EqProp's *other* big constraint (weight symmetry W=Wᵀ), which our substrate would otherwise owe.
- **EqProp beyond dissipative settling** — oscillator-network EqProp (arXiv:2504.11884), Hamiltonian Echo / Lagrangian EqProp (arXiv:2506.06248) — non-settling realizations if the loop isn't purely dissipative.
- **Scaling EqProp to deeper architectures** (arXiv:2509.26003) + Scellier 2024 fast nonlinear-resistive-network simulator (arXiv:2402.11674) — the depth frontier and the sim apparatus for any rung.
- **Quantum / Onsager-reciprocity EqProp** — Scellier 2024 (arXiv:2406.00879), Wanjura & Marquardt (arXiv:2406.06482) — the generalization of the two-phase rule beyond energy-based nets.
