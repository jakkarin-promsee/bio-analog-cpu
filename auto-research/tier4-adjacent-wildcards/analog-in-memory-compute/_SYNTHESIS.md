## The landscape (the honest map)

The field split, years ago, into an easy half and a hard half. The easy half — **analog inference** — is essentially solved at prototype scale: fully integrated multicore chips exist (Le Gallo 2023: 64 PCM cores, 14 nm, near-software ResNet/LSTM accuracy, ~10 TOPS/W measured), and their internal economy is exactly the one our meter assumes — the crossbar MACs are nearly free, the ADC/readout phase is the metered bottleneck (their 4-phase vs 1-phase modes trade precision for ~4× efficiency, our P8.4 ADC-bit sweep in silicon form). The hard half is **on-array update**. The founding spec analysis (Gokmen & Vlasov 2016) showed training needs ~1000 conductance states and up/down update asymmetry balanced to ~10% — and no resistive device meets that natively. The consequence is a decade-long mitigation industry with three roads: (1) **algorithmic compensation** — Tiki-Taka (2020) discovered asymmetry injects an *implicit cost term* into any gradient-following analog update and split the weight into a fast noisy integrator + slow clean store; its descendants TTv2 → c-TTv2/AGAD (Rasch 2024, Nature Comm) added digital filters, choppers, and dynamic references to keep in-array gradient accumulation alive (50–175× faster than digital accumulation); (2) **mixed-precision** — Nandakumar 2020 keeps a high-precision digital accumulator per weight and fires blind, granularity-metered pulses into the array (172× energy over 32-bit digital, at digital-traffic cost); (3) **rule simplification** — the only *fully integrated* learning chip in a flagship venue (Zhang 2023, Science: STELLAR) got there by reducing what the update asks of the device: sign/threshold-leaning updates, parallel tuning, adaptation-scoped learning.

Cutting across all three: the device that natively has the property everyone is fighting for — symmetric, linear, fine-grained, unlimited-endurance update — is the **capacitor**. IBM's VLSI 2018 cell (Li & Leobandung: trench capacitor + readout FET + two current-source FETs, 14 nm) holds the record for update symmetry/linearity, and the Nature 2018 training landmark (Ambrogio) reached software-equivalence *only* by pairing a volatile capacitor (takes every update) with PCM (holds the consolidated result), bridged by occasional polarity-inverted transfer. The capacitor's tax is volatility: retention τ must exceed ~10⁶ training cycles, and anything static leaks. Finally, the 2026 Nature Materials review (Jiang et al.) gives the current three-level error ledger — device (relaxation, telegraph noise, variation), array (IR drop, sneak paths — these scale with array *size*), system (ADC, depth-wise error accumulation) — and the field's verdict that shipped accuracy is always net-of-mitigation.

## How WE differ — the validated / harder-than-admitted ledger

**Validated by the hardware reality:**
- **Resident-weight CIM + the crossbar MVM floor** — fully fabricated fact, not a bet. Our ADC-centred behavioral meter has the right shape (HERMES is its silicon embodiment), and our 5.4× substrate factor reads *conservative* against field claims (Nandakumar 172×, Ambrogio ~280× projections, our own ≥2.7× floor logic).
- **The Scap** — the strongest single validation in this sweep. The capacitor is not our naive stand-in for a "real" memristor; it is the field's *best-in-class* update element, chosen by IBM for exactly the property (symmetric fine update, unbounded endurance) that kills RRAM/PCM training. Our always-plastic SCFF bulk sits in the capacitor's *friendly* regime: continuous learning is continuous refresh, so leakage is absorbed (Li 2018 makes this argument explicitly).
- **The architecture philosophy** — every road that works simplifies the update (signs, thresholds, closed-form, rare precise transfers) and spends digital budget only where structurally needed. Our 80/20, closed-form namer, SRAM sign bits, and gated rare consolidation are the same doctrine the hardware field converged on independently. Ambrogio's capacitor-fast/PCM-slow cell is literally our awake/sleep economy in device form.

**Harder than the math model admits:**
1. **The update primitive is not free.** Our simulator's exact `w += Δw` hides the layer where analog training actually dies. Even capacitors have update asymmetries (current-source mismatch, charge injection), and Tiki-Taka's implicit-cost-term result applies to *any* incremental analog rule — including SCFF's. Whether InfoNCE-driven clustering self-corrects or compounds under asymmetric/granular updates is **unmeasured by anyone** — it is our realism pass's experiment #1, and (if SCFF tolerates it) potentially a publishable claim.
2. **Volatility vs anything static.** The Scap holds only what keeps being rewritten. The LUT/prototype store and any frozen weights need SRAM/NVM or refresh discipline — our LUT-in-SRAM choice is load-bearing, not incidental; and nonvolatile analog stores bring their own tax (PCM conductance drift on stored prototypes).
3. **Array-level physics bounds scale.** IR drop, parasitics, and sneak paths grow with array size — P11's "substrate factor grows 5.4→7.4× with width" extrapolation rides on arrays that get *harder* at width. Real chips also spend ~8T4R per weight (HERMES), an order more area/complexity than one-Scap-per-weight.
4. **Continuous every-step plasticity is beyond demonstrated practice.** Fabricated learning demos are episodic adaptation (STELLAR) or bounded training runs (Ambrogio); no one has shipped a lifetime always-updating analog array. Capacitors dodge the endurance wall that would make this impossible on filamentary devices — but the regime is still unexplored territory, not established ground.

## The gap / what we haven't tried
- **SCFF-under-update-non-ideality sweep** (asymmetry α_up≠α_down, per-cell spread, Δg granularity/states, update noise) using the RPU tolerance-sweep methodology; AIHWKIT device models are a ready harness.
- **Chopping/polarity inversion** written into the Scap update spec pre-emptively (cheap, general, cancels systematic sign asymmetry).
- **ε-metered blind writes** (Nandakumar's threshold-transfer) as the discipline for sleep-time consolidation into analog/NVM stores.
- **IR-drop-aware width check** on the P11 scaling claim; depth-wise analog error accumulation through the 12-layer bulk (never simulated).
- **Attractor-settle recall** (mem-HNN) vs matchline CAM (Li 2020) as the hippocampus read primitive — partial-cue completion for free, at the price of capacity/interference management.
