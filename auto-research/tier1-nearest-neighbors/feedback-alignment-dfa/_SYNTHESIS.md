# Synthesis — Feedback Alignment / DFA / Target Propagation  (Tier 1, t1.6)
**The question:** This is the *other* way to drop backprop (vs our forward-only SCFF). Where does DFA/FA beat or lose to the Forward-Forward family and to us? Ours pays **zero backward path**; FA/DFA still needs a random backward projection. Which is the better fit for an analog crossbar, and what does the DFA-scaling-failure literature imply for anyone dropping exact gradients?

**Already in `draft6.0/research/`:** `research/survey/feedback-alignment.detail.md` covers the anchors well — Lillicrap FA 2016, Nøkland DFA 2016, the weight-transport argument, the alignment-angle-<90° diagnostic, and the MNIST→CIFAR→ImageNet accuracy ladder (incl. the Bartunov ceiling qualitatively). This sweep goes **broader/newer**: the *theory* of why alignment happens and breaks (Refinetti 2021), the *scaling counter-narrative* (Launay 2020, DFA on transformers/NeRF/recommenders), the *analog-hardware realizations* (Filipovich photonic 2022; reservoir/optical DFA 2024–2025), and the *forward-only bridge* (Frenkel DRTP) + the target-prop branch (Lee DTP).

## The landscape

**Three camps, one shared premise.** All of this family accepts that backprop's **weight transport** (the transposed backward crossbar) and its **serialized backward pass** are the un-physical, hardware-expensive parts — and each removes them differently. (a) **Feedback Alignment** keeps the layer-by-layer backward chain but swaps each `Wᵀ` for a *fixed random* `B` (Lillicrap). (b) **Direct FA** deletes the chain and broadcasts the output error *directly* to every layer through per-layer random matrices, fully parallel (Nøkland). (c) **Target propagation** carries *targets* (not gradients) downward through *trained inverses*, with a difference-correction to stabilize the imperfect inversion (Lee/Bengio). DRTP (Frenkel) is the family's extreme: use the **label as an error-sign proxy** and project it forward, so there is **no backward pass at all** — but it stays supervised.

**Why it works, and where it breaks.** The rescue mechanism is *alignment*: forward weights rotate until the random feedback becomes a descent direction (angle < 90°). Refinetti 2021 gives the mechanistic theory — training is **"align, then memorise,"** and the master variable is the **conditioning of the alignment matrices**. That single result explains the family's signature failure: **weight sharing (convolution) wrecks conditioning, so DFA can't align, so it fails on CNNs and at ImageNet scale** (Bartunov 2018's matched-budget audit is the empirical version). Launay 2020 is the crucial nuance: "fails to scale" is really "fails on *conv*" — DFA trains transformers, NeRF, recommenders, and graph nets *close to* fine-tuned backprop, because those have **no weight sharing**.

**The hardware camp is where DFA genuinely wins.** DFA's direct random projection is a *static, parallel broadcast*, which is exactly what analog crossbars do cheaply and what backprop's transpose does not. Filipovich 2022 trains MNIST **in situ** on a silicon-photonic microring mesh at **<1 pJ/MAC and trillions of MAC/s**; reservoir-DFA (Nakajima, Comms Physics 2024) computes the random projection with *physical dynamics*; optical DFA scaling continues into 2025 (PNAS). DFA is the **more hardware-mature** backprop-free training rule today.

## How WE differ  ← the money section

Our SCFF sits at a **different extreme on two axes at once**, and stating both is what places us honestly:

1. **Backward path.** FA/DFA keep a *random backward projection* (an error/label broadcast + a per-layer random matrix multiply). We keep **none** — credit is a local windowed InfoNCE contrast, derivative-free, no global error, no broadcast network. DRTP is the only family member that also removes the backward path — but it does so by keeping the **label at every layer**.
2. **Supervision.** The entire FA/DFA/TP/DRTP family is **supervised** — it needs the global error or the label to teach the hidden layers. Our 80% bulk is **label-free**; labels enter only at the tiny closed-form namer. So our genuinely novel combination is **not** "no backward path" (DRTP has that) and **not** "no transpose" (all of them have that) — it is **"no backward path AND no supervision in the bulk, on a flat-vector substrate."** That precise phrasing is the contribution; anything looser is re-invention.

**What the scaling literature says about *our* locality gap — the load-bearing lesson.** Bartunov + Refinetti prove a law that binds us too: **drop the exact gradient direction and you pay a task-dependent tax that grows with difficulty, depth, and structure.** This is *exactly* the shape of our own P4/P10/P11 findings — we win continual/noise/nuisance-robustness and **trail on static accuracy and many-class**, "by design." The FA family is the published, backprop-adjacent confirmation that this is a **family property of non-exact-gradient learning**, not a defect unique to SCFF. Two consolations are real: (i) our substrate is **flat-vector — no weight sharing**, so we sit on the *good* side of the conditioning line that kills DFA on conv (Launay/Refinetti); (ii) our Phase-5 depth-decay is our version of "alignment can't complete," and we cured it with a **sharper forward objective + bounded window**, not by chasing a random backward matrix — a structurally cleaner fix.

**Which is the better fit for an analog crossbar?** On pure locality, **we win** — zero backward path beats a random backward path; no second projection network, no error fan-out, no supervision wiring. But on *maturity*, **DFA wins today** — it is built and measured on real analog silicon (Filipovich), training-capable at <1 pJ/MAC, while our forward-only contrast on an analog substrate is **designed, not built** (our own §2.4 flags the InfoNCE softmax/normalizer and LUT-negative gathering as the unbuilt, non-crossbar-native pieces). The honest placement: we are the *more local* design; DFA is the *more demonstrated* one. Our edge is only real if the unbuilt bridge (streamed negatives + on-chip contrast) turns out to be cheaper than DFA's error-broadcast network — an open, Stage-2 question.

## The gap / what we haven't tried
- **A DRTP-style head vs our closed-form namer.** DRTP is the FA-lineage's *feedforward, fixed-random-projection supervised head*. Our namer is the analytic-CL lineage's answer (SLDA/RanPAC). A head-level bake-off (DRTP-random-projection vs SLDA) is a concrete, cheap, untested probe — and it lives exactly where a random backward path *might* actually help us (the 20%, not the bulk).
- **The alignment/conditioning diagnostic on our bulk.** Refinetti's "conditioning of alignment matrices" is an instrument; adapting a conditioning measure to our per-window credit could tell us whether our depth-decay is the *same* pathology as DFA's conv failure or a distinct one.
- **Honest energy accounting against DFA-on-crossbar.** Filipovich publishes pJ/MAC and MAC/s for a *training-capable* analog crossbar. Our Stage-2 analog-realism pass owes a comparison in those units — the only way to substantiate "more local ⇒ cheaper" over DFA's demonstrated numbers.
- **A DFA broadcast for a future non-conv north-star block.** If the recurrent/attention north star ever needs a credit path SCFF's local contrast can't reach, Launay shows DFA aligns fine on non-conv blocks — a cheaper-than-backprop option *for the parts SCFF can't organize*, kept off the bulk.
