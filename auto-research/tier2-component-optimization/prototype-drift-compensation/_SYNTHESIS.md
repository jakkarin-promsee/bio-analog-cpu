# Synthesis — prototype / covariance drift compensation  (Tier 2, t2.6)
**The question:** Does closed-form prototype/covariance drift compensation beat our periodic full closed-form re-fit — is it cheaper, or safer between sleeps (the P10 REV-staircase stale-namer gap)?
**Already in `draft6.0/research/`:** The bulk/head-split diagnosis is already ours (P9.0: the representation *rotates*, the head statistics *rot* — not forgetting). The raw-store re-anchor is already deployed (P9.4 proto-reanchor: retention 0.787→0.986 by re-forwarding the LUT at sleep). AdaGauss (t1.2) already banks the covariance-goes-stale-under-drift bridge; EASE (t1.5) already banks the similarity-weighted prototype-reconstruction patch for the REV staircase. This card set adds the **compensation family proper** (SDC → LDC → ADC → EFC) plus the freeze-and-translate control (FeTrIL) that the curated library did not hold.

## The landscape (2–4 paragraphs)
Exemplar-free class-incremental learning (EFCIL) has a signature failure: a feature extractor that keeps training moves old classes' stored prototypes into a dead coordinate frame — the head points where classes *used to live*. Because no old exemplars are stored, you cannot re-measure the old classes directly. The whole "drift compensation" line is a decade of increasingly clever ways to *estimate* where the old prototypes went using only data you legally have.

The estimators form a clean accuracy/cost ladder. **SDC (Yu 2020)** is the seminal move: measure the displacement field δ = f_t(x) − f_{t−1}(x) on *current* data, then transfer it to old prototypes by a kernel-weighted (proximity) average — closed-form, no training, one weighted average. Its load-bearing assumption is that the drift field is *smooth*. **LDC (Gomez-Villa 2024)** replaces the smoothing with a *learned* projector P_t (gradient-fit to regress f_{t−1}→f_t), which captures class-dependent, non-smooth drift and — critically — is the only method in the line validated on a moving *unsupervised* backbone, the regime closest to SCFF. **ADC (Goswami 2024)** is the accuracy ceiling: it *manufactures* probes exactly at old-class locations by adversarially perturbing new images until the old model embeds them at μ_c, then reads where the new model sends them — most accurate, but the probes need input-gradients through the network.

Two papers sit off the estimator ladder. **FeTrIL (Petit 2023)** refuses drift entirely: freeze the backbone after task 1 (drift = 0 by construction), store one centroid per class, and synthesize old-class training features by *translating* real new-class features by the centroid difference (borrow the spread, correct only the mean). It beats ten heavier moving-backbone methods on static benchmarks — the uncomfortable result that a good frozen first-task backbone plus mean-corrected borrowed spread is hard to beat. **EFC (Magistri 2024)** *shapes* drift while it happens: an Empirical Feature Matrix (a tractable second-order feature-space sensitivity metric) says which feature directions old tasks care about, and the backbone is regularized to drift freely in cheap directions and resist drift along expensive ones. EFC++ then splits off a post-training *prototype re-balancing* phase — which is our sleep, rediscovered in the gradient world.

## How WE differ  ← the money section
**We are the only object in this landscape that legally holds the old data.** Every method here is exemplar-free *by constraint* — SDC's kernel transfer, LDC's learned projector, and ADC's adversarial probes all exist to **approximate a measurement we can just take**. At sleep we re-forward the bounded raw-exemplar LUT through the current bulk and re-anchor the prototypes *exactly* (P9.4). That full re-fit is not a competitor to drift compensation — it is the ground truth these estimators are trying to reach. So the direct verdict on the topic question is:

> **Drift compensation does NOT beat our periodic full re-fit on accuracy — our sleep re-fit is the exact answer the compensation family only estimates.** Its value for us is not *replacement*; it is *interpolation between sleeps*, and *cadence relief*.

That is the real gap. The full re-fit is correct but **discrete** — it only happens at sleep. Between sleeps the bulk keeps rotating, and SLDA's `partial_fit` only keeps *streaming* classes' prototypes fresh; prototypes of classes **absent from the current domain** go stale. That is exactly the **P10 REV staircase** ("namer frame goes stale between sleeps"), currently paid for with a tight cadence (grid-4, GD-share 0.178). Drift compensation is a *between-sleeps closed-form nudge* that could flatten the staircase and let cadence relax back toward grid-8 — cheaper *per unit time*, without touching the bulk. So: **not cheaper than a single re-fit; potentially much cheaper than re-fitting *often enough to stay fresh*, which is what we actually do now.**

Where we are genuinely re-inventing vs genuinely ahead:
- **Re-invention (mild confirmation):** EFC++'s split of "train, then re-balance prototypes" is our wake/sleep split found from the gradient side. SDC's own second finding (embedding/representation forgetting ≪ classifier forgetting) is our P9.0 verdict. We are not novel here — we are *confirmed*.
- **Genuinely ours:** the raw-store LUT. ADC pays with input-gradients and FeTrIL pays with total stability to dodge the fact that they can't see old classes; we pay with a little SRAM and see them for real. The field's own trajectory (SDC→LDC→ADC, each more elaborate) is an argument *for* our engineering choice, not against it.
- **The threat we must answer:** FeTrIL. On *static* arenas, freeze-everything + translate beats careful drift-tracking. Our P11 answer is that the always-learning bulk wins *real coherent drift* (gas arena) — but that means every t2.6 cell must clear the FeTrIL bar to prove the drift-tracking earns its cost.

## The gap / what we haven't tried
Ranked by expected payoff, each tagged for substrate-fit — **[store]** = needs a raw-data store (we have the LUT), **[CF]** = closed-form / no gradient, **[bnd]** = bounded cost:

1. **SDC-between-sleeps nudge on absent-class prototypes** — ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐. **[CF][bnd]**, *no new store*. Between sleeps, compute per-class displacement of the fresh running means (SLDA `partial_fit`) vs the stored prototypes of *present* classes, then kernel-weight-transfer that motion to the *absent*-class prototypes untouched by the current domain. Uses features the awake namer already computes — no extra forwards, no store, one weighted average per fire. **Test:** does it flatten the REV staircase at grid-8 and buy cadence back (GD-share 0.178 → lower) at matched retention? This is the single highest-value lever in the topic.
2. **Closed-form LDC projector (ridge, not gradient)** — ⭐⭐⭐. **[CF][bnd]**, *light store* (a feature cache of LUT items at last sleep, not raw pixels twice). Fit a *linear* P_t by least-squares on (LUT-feature-at-last-sleep, current-LUT-feature) pairs — one solve — and push stale prototypes through it. Captures class-dependent, non-smooth drift that SDC's kernel smoothing misses. **Only worth a cell if the SDC nudge fails on SCFF drift** — and LDC's own evidence (kernel transfer underperforms a fitted map on *unsupervised* backbones) warns it might, since SCFF drift is endogenous/unsupervised.
3. **EFM-weighted drift *meter* (not a brake)** — ⭐⭐⭐. **[CF][bnd]**, *no new store*. Project measured tap-drift onto the SLDA prototype-difference (class-discriminative) directions — closed-form from stats we already store — so the between-sleeps staleness alarm fires on drift that *matters* and ignores rotation in dead directions. A strictly better *trigger feature* for the deferred tap-drift gate (commit ef80d1f); not a compensator, an upgrade to *when* we compensate/sleep.
4. **Micro-sleep fallback for far-domain absent classes** — ⭐⭐⭐. **[store][CF][bnd]**. When the current domain is *far* from an absent class, the SDC nudge correctly but uselessly does nothing (weights vanish). The honest patch is a *micro-probe*: re-forward a few LUT items of that one stale class through the current bulk (~probe_size × depth forwards, ≈100× cheaper than a full re-fit). This is ADC's *judgment* — "measure where you can't see" — done with real LUT probes instead of adversarial ones (which are substrate-illegal anyway: no backward path through the forward-only bulk).
5. **FeTrIL translation for cap relief + the mandatory sanity control** — ⭐⭐⭐. **[CF][bnd]**, *no new store*. (a) At sleep, densify thin CBRS classes (P9.3: cap must scale with #classes) with translated pseudo-features from feature-rich classes — one translation per synthetic sample, borrow spread, correct mean. (b) **Control condition, not optional:** every t2.6 cell above must beat "freeze taps at birth + FeTrIL translation" to justify tracking drift at all.

**Dropped as unadoptable:** ADC's adversarial-probe *mechanism* (input-gradients through the bulk — structurally impossible on forward-only analog physics) and EFC's elastic *regularizer* (modifies the bulk's committed SCFF objective by gradient; P9.1 already struck the drift-slowdown N2 lever family). Both are kept only for their *judgments* (estimator accuracy is the separating axis; not all drift directions matter), which feed items 3–4.
