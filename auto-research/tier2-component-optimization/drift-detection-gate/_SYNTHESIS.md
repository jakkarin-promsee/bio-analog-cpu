# Synthesis — Concept-drift detection beyond DDM: the awake-gate trigger  (Tier 2, t2.4)
**The question:** For our gate, what detector is a better awake-trigger than error-EMA DDM — earlier, label-free, or direction-aware — while staying cheap + closed-form + analog-substrate-friendly? Would a representation-drift (feature-space) trigger beat the error-rate trigger (P8.2's spine-clean tap-drift lead)?
**Already in `draft6.0/research/`:** `papers/phase8/` covers DDM, ADWIN, the correct ADWIN-U citation, Skip-RNN's budget loss, the MTD/MTFA/FAR eval vocabulary, the SDC/LDC road-not-taken, and a brief DriftLens mention. This sweep goes past that: the unsupervised/representation-detection literature proper (STUDD, D3, MCD-DD, UDD, DriftLens in mechanism depth), drift-*magnitude* estimation (Webb), the theory objection (Hinder et al.), and the cost-aware-cadence line (Cara, Regol) that t2.3 flagged.

## The landscape (the streaming-systems view)

The field has stratified into four camps. **(1) Error-rate detectors** (DDM, EDDM, HDDM, ADWIN-on-error) — the 20-year-old core, statistically clean, but structurally *late* (an error must happen before it can be counted) and label-hungry (someone must supply the true label every step). **(2) Label-free signal engineering** — keep the classic detector, change what it eats: student-teacher disagreement (STUDD), model uncertainty (UDD), discriminator AUC on raw features (D3). The pattern is explicit in STUDD and UDD: *the detector layer is solved; the contribution is always the signal*. **(3) Representation-drift detectors** — measure distribution distance in a learned embedding: per-label Gaussian distances (DriftLens), learned-kernel MMD (MCD-DD). This camp is the newest (2024–25) and the closest to our unshipped tap-drift trigger; DriftLens is its deployable exemplar and *already validated our direction signal in P8.2*. **(4) The economists** — Cara and Regol et al. argue the trigger question itself is mis-posed: don't detect *change*, integrate *cost* (staleness debt vs retrain cost) or forecast the performance trajectory; both beat detection-triggered retraining in their tests.

Over all of it hangs Hinder/Vaquet/Hammer's theory result: from `P(X)` alone, harmful and harmless drift are **indistinguishable** — every raw-feature detector (D3, KSWIN-on-features, plain MMD) owns an irreducible false-alarm class. The field's standard escape is model-conditioning (loss surrogates, uncertainty). Our P8.2 result is a *third* escape the taxonomy doesn't name: project feature drift onto the **class-direction subspace** the namer maintains, making nuisance drift geometrically invisible (0.84× baseline) while the raw-magnitude null false-fires at FAR 0.938. That is a genuinely defensible position — but it is currently a validated measurement, not a shipped gate.

## How WE differ  ← the money section

**What's genuinely ours:** (a) the *safety* framing — everyone else prices a false fire in compute; we measured that firing more **forgets more** (worst-BWT −0.137 always-pay vs 0.000 gated), which makes FAR a correctness axis, not a cost axis — no paper here has that inversion; (b) the metered energy account (pJ per fire) that turns the economists' assumed cost ratios into a measured number; (c) the class-direction projection as the harmful/harmless filter (P8.2), which DriftLens approximates per-label but nobody states as subspace-projection-vs-nuisance.

**What's a re-invention:** our tap-drift trigger is representation-drift detection (camp 3) — DriftLens is its published form, and D3/MMD are its ancestors. Our "the gate and sleep-cadence are the same detector at two timescales" intuition is the field's monitoring-vs-triggering split.

**What's a gap — where the literature is ahead of us:** (1) the deployed gate is **label-hungry** (error-EMA needs true labels every step) — camp 2 solved that shape-adapter problem three ways, one of which (disagreement) costs us almost nothing; (2) the gate is **binary** — no magnitude (Webb, DriftLens's drift curve), so the sleep response can't be proportional; (3) the cadence is **open-loop** (grid-4) — the economists close that loop and claim wins over detection-triggered firing; (4) "DDM consumes an error rate" was treated as a blocker for shipping the direction trigger — the detector-menu (ADWIN/KSWIN/Page-Hinkley/calibrated threshold) dissolves it.

## The gap / what we haven't tried (ranked, with substrate cost)

1. **Self-disagreement trigger (STUDD-shaped, zero new learners).** Keep the last sleep's namer weights frozen (one C×d matrix resident in the crossbar); per-step disagreement between live and snapshot argmax = a label-free Bernoulli stream → feed the **unmodified committed DDM**. Ships a label-free gate with no detector change; refresh the snapshot at each sleep. *Substrate: cheap — one extra crossbar read + one comparator.* Test in the frozen P8.2 harness vs MTD 6 (tap) / 14 (error).
2. **Ship the validated tap-drift direction trigger through a continuous-signal detector.** The blocker was signal shape, not validity: ADWIN, Page-Hinkley (a leaky integrator + threshold — the most analog-native detector in the entire literature), KSWIN, or a DriftLens-style offline-calibrated threshold all consume the continuous direction statistic directly. *Substrate: cheap (PH/EMA) to moderate (KSWIN's order statistics).*
3. **Margin-EMA gate (UDD-shaped, closed-form uncertainty).** SLDA's top1−top2 margin is free at every naming; ADWIN/PH on the margin stream = label-free, model-conditioned, no sampling (our answer to MC-Dropout). Expected to sit between error (late) and tap (early) on MTD. *Substrate: cheap — the margin is already computed.*
4. **Drift-magnitude → proportional sleep (Webb + DriftLens's drift curve).** Integrate the tap statistic between sleeps as a path-length magnitude; size λ_ema / LUT re-fit fraction / cadence multiplier by it. Directly attacks P8's named caveat that the committed cadence is drift-rate-conditional with nothing watching the rate. *Substrate: cheap — an accumulator.*
5. **Error-debt cadence (Cara-shaped).** Replace grid-4 with "sleep when accumulated error-EMA excess ≥ metered sleep cost" — one signal serving gate + cadence + (per t2.3 InfoRS) buffer eviction. Guard rail: the P10 cliff map (safety plunge past interval ~6 on the home) bounds the debt threshold. *Substrate: cheap — an integrator + comparator; the risk is safety, not cost.*
6. **Harmful-drift filter for feature detectors (D3+Fisher hybrid).** Closed-form LDA old-vs-new discriminant on a tap window; cosine of its direction against the namer's class directions decides fire (aligned = harmful) vs ignore (orthogonal = nuisance). Also yields *which class moved* → class-conditional partial sleep. *Substrate: moderate — windowed means + one crossbar read.*
7. **Broader nuisance battery before shipping any feature trigger (Hinder).** Our harmless-drift evidence is one covariate-shift geometry; the survey's parametric drift suite + the adversarial-attacks paper supply the red-team set. *Substrate: free — sim-only validation.*

**The direct answer to the topic question:** yes — a label-free trigger should beat the deployed error-EMA DDM on detection latency (P8.2 already measured the 8-step lead), and the representation-drift literature (DriftLens) plus the detector menu removes the only shipping blocker. But the *cheapest correct first step* is not swapping the detector — it is the self-disagreement signal (lever 1), which keeps the committed, validated DDM and removes the label dependency; the direction trigger (lever 2) is the validated latency upgrade behind it; and any of these must be judged on **FAR × forgetting**, not MTD alone, because in our economy a false fire is a safety event.

## Leads spawned
- **Page-Hinkley as an analog primitive** — a leaky integrator + comparator *is* PH; the one detector that could be a circuit, not an algorithm (feeds the analog-realism pass).
- **Drift localization → class-conditional partial sleep** (Hinder Part B + DriftLens per-label distances): re-fit only the classes that moved.
- **Adversarial robustness of drift gates** (arXiv 2411.16591): can a stream be crafted that never trips our gate while degrading the namer? Safety-relevant given firing-more-forgets-more.
- **Open-world / novel-class drift** — the class-direction projection is blind to classes it has no direction for; MCD-DD's global discrepancy or OCDD as the companion statistic.
- **Verification-latency literature** (labels arrive late, not never) — the realistic label model between our labeled home stream and fully-unsupervised deployment.
- **Classifier two-sample tests** (Lopez-Paz & Oquab 2017) — the theory under D3, for the hybrid's null calibration.
