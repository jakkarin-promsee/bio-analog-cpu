## The whole thing in one breath

Two brains. A cheap, unsupervised **SCFF** front (~80%) that organizes the world for free, and a small, precise **gradient-descent** back (~20%) that puts real labels on it — because direction is the one expensive thing in learning, so we pay for it *once*, where it counts. The two are chained as **residual boosting blocks**: each block a weak corrector, SCFF doing feature work inside the residual stream, a GD checkpoint at each block's exit. Learning is **threshold-gated** (cheap local SCFF most of the time; expensive direction only when the cheap path stalls) and **sleep-consolidated** (periodic full-batch GD over a prototype memory, so nothing rots).

## The net — the three approved calls

**N1 · SCFF stays the cheap brain.** Vanilla, unsupervised — the only rule that is local + derivative-free + forward-only + unsupervised at once. Relabel our **summation** form as *our* reformulation of the paper's concatenation (exact because `W₁=W₂`; first-layer-only; it's what makes mono-forward possible). Keep Distance-Forward's **margin loss on standby** for if the log-loss is mushy. → [scff.md](../research/papers/phase1-2/scff.md), [distance-forward.md](../research/papers/phase1-2/distance-forward.md)

**N2 · The middle layer = stability + coordination, split apart.** Don't blend two objectives on one layer — they fight. Instead: **plasticity-gradient slowdown** (mirror LLRD — slow the *late* SCFF layers GD reads) for stability, plus **DF-O overlapping blocks** for coordination. **EMA-view** (BYOL momentum encoder) is the de-risked upgrade if the cheap slowdown pinches SCFF's learning. → [llrd.md](../research/papers/phase1-2/llrd.md), [distance-forward.md](../research/papers/phase1-2/distance-forward.md), [byol.md](../research/papers/phase1-2/byol.md)

**N3 · GD = residual boosting blocks.** Each GD checkpoint fits the *residual* the previous one left (boosting telescoping sum — BoostResNet); SCFF does feature work inside the residual stream. The boosting guarantee lives on the labels → in the GD checkpoints, not the unsupervised SCFF stages. → [boostresnet.md](../research/papers/phase1-2/boostresnet.md)

## Supporting structure (committed)

**S1 · Path diversity comes from depth, not input-width.** Concatenation's only edge over summation was path diversity (training-time over-parameterization) — and it's *redundant* diversity that collapses to `W₁=W₂`. Depth buys it better: non-redundant capacity, uniform width-`d` rails, and it matches our own locked "path-diversity-per-scap" topology. Depth's cost is coordination debt — which N2 + N3 already exist to pay down.

**S2 · Mono-forward, dual-rail.** One forward sweep, two worlds (positive + negative) side by side through the *shared* weight crossbar. Doubles only the **LocalCapacitor** activation buffers (RAM-like temp `a` values), **not** the Scaps. Trades cheap buffer area for one charge cycle instead of two. Both goodnesses stay explicit → full two-sided loss, no scale runaway.

**S3 · GD reads via taps.** GD reads the last `n` SCFF layers (richer than one thin top layer; precedented by SCFF's own all-layer readout). GD reads, never writes into SCFF → no shared weights, and a built-in stop-gradient (BYOL's anti-collapse condition, for free).

**S4 · Two GD organs.** *Interface GD* (small, per-block exit, tracking job, SGD+momentum, squared error) vs *Output GD* (the brain, final, 2–3 layer residual MLP, Adam-class online / full-batch at sleep, cross-entropy).

**S5 · Inter-layer normalization, mandatory.** Required by FF for correctness (the next layer can't just recycle the previous layer's goodness) — and now load-bearing twice, because it's also the guard against the residual shortcut (a block inflating `‖h‖²` just by passing its input through).

**S6 · Threshold-gated learning.** Loss < θ → SCFF only (cheap, local). Loss ≥ θ → SCFF + chained GD delta. Justified by the gradient-mismatch ε-floor: local learning converges while block disagreement is small, stalls when it dominates. Candidate refinement: gate on loss-*slope* (plateau detection), not absolute loss.

**S7 · Sleep consolidation.** Periodic full-batch GD over history, re-covering the whole data range against the *current* SCFF map. Cheap by construction (GD ≈ 20% of weights, SCFF body frozen). Robustness from tap-normalization + drift-spanning replay + free analog noise augmentation.

**S8 · LUT prototype memory.** Deduplicated raw-input prototypes (winner-take-all novelty allocation). One store, three customers: SCFF negatives, sleep replay, and the seed of the future "memory model" (the hippocampus track). Experiment 3.2 becomes the prototype-history cell.

**S9 · Readout = a fixed short-stack placement (Phase-5 close-out; revises S3's literal "tap ALL layers", keeps its intent).** SCFF composes the depth a task needs (sharper InfoNCE temp 0.2 — the *earn* lever), and that depth is **read by a short, fixed truncation stack at the extractor end** (~L2–3 on the flat continual home; all-tap when peak accuracy is wanted; the deep L12 bulk reserved for compositional tasks). The **adaptive per-sample early-exit was struck** (P5.5 — the flat home rewards pooling over placement) and **no frozen residual** is carried (P5.6 skipped). S3's intent — *read multiple layers, GD never writes SCFF* — is kept; what changes is *where* to read: the sharp extractor depth, not literally every layer (all-tap dilutes the class signal with the drifted deep layers). → [`../src/phase5/README.md`](../src/phase5/README.md)

**S10 · SCFF carries a noise-aware objective (Phase-6 close-out; makes S7's "free analog noise augmentation" a committed *base* property, not a readout-side afterthought).** The base representation is hardened against analog/continual noise **in the SCFF objective, forward-only** — by corrupting **one InfoNCE view with broad (iid) noise at train time** (`NoiseAugContrast`, σ_aug=1.0). This is load-bearing because a frozen readout *preserves but cannot manufacture* backbone robustness (LP-FT), so robustness that isn't in the base can never be added by the 20% GD namer. **Scoped:** it substantially (partially) hardens the Rasch-dominant **tap** channel (retention 0.82→0.87, 5/5 paired), is **continual-safe** (the A6 win survives — BWT −0.022→−0.017) and **natural-data-confirmed**, but leaves a named **input-transducer directional residual** for Stage-2 read-side (calibration under shift; ADC<3-bit). Two Phase-6 corrections to prior intuition: the effective augmentation is **generic, not directional-specific** (broad iid ≥ random-axis > a directional-specific corruption — you cannot bet on one axis for a coherent shift unknown at train time), and the directional enemy is a **coherent translation** along the class axis (per-sample rep-cos is blind to it → **retention** is the direction-first read). Door B (the continual "all data is noise") is resolved: the class direction **forms from a fully-noisy stream** (buffer purity ≈ naive averaging, unneeded here). → [`../src/phase6/README.md`](../src/phase6/README.md)

**S11 · The GD namer is a closed-form/streaming analytic head, not gradient descent (Phase-7 close-out; SUPERSEDES N3, COLLAPSES S4, EXTENDS S9).** The ~20% "precise brain" that maps the frozen SCFF bulk → our labels is a **bake-off winner, not a GD head by assumption**: the committed namer is **RanPAC** (a frozen random ReLU projection → running-Gram ridge prototype, `W=(G+λI)⁻¹M`) **+ a class-balanced-reservoir imbalance guard** — **no gradient, closed-form, streaming.** On the continual home it ties the gradient MLP anchor on accuracy×BWT (a 3-way statistical tie {MLP, RanPAC, SLDA}, all no-gradient but MLP), leads on natural digits (0.949, #1, near-zero forgetting), passes the A6 gate (the trained cosine-softmax **and** the max-magnitude FeCAM are struck for denting A6 — the *trained weights*, not the metric, carry the recency bias), and is the most spine-clean of the tied cluster. **So the "20% GD" is a *role*, not a *method* — the 20% is not even gradient descent.** **The spine bends (honest):** the direction-pure cosine head is spine-clean (argmax-flip 0.000) but sub-competitive where the bulk has structure (**magnitude-wins-spine-bends**, Δ=0.128 synthetic; the price shrinks 4× to −0.036 on digits and ≈0 on CIFAR-flat) — the winner reads a magnitude (ridge) yet is recency-robust by having no trained weights (recency-robust ≠ direction-reading). **Two Phase-7 corrections to the plan's intuition:** the imbalance guard is **class-balanced reservoir (buffer-side), NOT AIR** (AIR over-corrects); and the "multimodality cliff" is **anisotropy, not multimodality** (natural features are unimodal — the +0.19 lever is a *tied covariance*, closed-form, no non-convex mixture). **Consequences for the earlier record:** **N3** ("GD = residual boosting blocks") is **superseded** by *single frozen bulk + read-only namer* (boosting dropped, the P2.5 forward-leak wall); **S4** ("two GD organs") **collapses to one** (the namer; Interface-GD retired with the blocks); **S9** (fixed short-stack reader) is **extended** with the committed *head* (RanPAC/analytic), not just the placement. **Cost caveat → Phase 8:** RanPAC's projection proxy-cost is ~200× SLDA's — **SLDA is the cheaper within-noise no-gradient fallback** (passes the gate, more robust on depth-less inputs); cost was descriptive-only here, P8 prices it. → [`../src/phase7/README.md`](../src/phase7/README.md)

**S12 · The economy is drift-gated, and the gate is a *safety* mechanism — the two-brain loop is continual-safe run LIVE (Phase-8 close-out; RESOLVES S6, RESOLVES S11's cost caveat, EXTENDS S7, discharges the 80/20-proxy tag).** Both brains were run **live together** for the first time (SCFF learns forward-only on every input; the namer tracks the drifting taps via an awake gate + periodic sleep) on a CI+covariate-nuisance stream, on a new streaming **`partial_fit`** primitive (running Gram `(G,M)` + `λ_ema`, guarded ≡ a batch fit to 4e-15). **The committed economy:** deployed head **SLDA** · awake gate **DDM** (two-threshold error) · trigger **class-direction tap-drift** · sleep **grid-8 / full LUT history / λ_ema 1.0** · guard **cbrs** · envelope unchanged (GD reads taps, never writes SCFF). **The metered 80/20 is real, not a proxy:** with the gate on, the GD namer is **12.1% of total substrate energy** (GD-share 0.121 ≤ 0.25 on a behavioral ADC-centred meter); off, 77.8% (the gate *creates* the split). **OURS ≈ half the energy of BP+replay** at matched retention on the same substrate table (bp_ratio 0.501). **LIVE-SAFE:** the assembled loop holds worst-point (pre-sleep) BWT at **0.000** (0/5 seeds regress vs the known-boundary oracle) at oracle-level accuracy. **The crux (a new supporting decision):** **firing more forgets more** — always-pay (namer every step) forgets (−0.137) by chasing the recency-skewed stream, while the disciplined economy (rare gated fires + cbrs + full-LUT sleep) does not — **the gate is a safety mechanism, not just a cost saver.** **The spine, demonstrated:** the trigger fires on class **direction** (invariant to the nuisance covariate, 0.84×) not **magnitude** (the null spikes 10× and false-fires on 94% of nuisance steps — density≠class, 8th coat). **Two Phase-8 corrections to the plan's intuition:** the gate's value is **safety, not just cost**; and **regular cadence beats boundary-aligned sleep** (grid worst-BWT 0.000 vs oracle-boundary −0.439, because the worst point falls mid-segment). **Consequences for the earlier record:** **S6** (threshold-gated learning) **resolves** to DDM + a class-direction tap-drift trigger; **S11's cost caveat resolves** to **commit SLDA** (metered 69× cheaper namer, AA ties/beats live — RanPAC kept as the accuracy/spine reference, not deployed); **S7** (sleep) is **extended** to the grid-8 / full-history / λ_ema-1.0 detector-driven cadence (was oracle-boundary). **Still owed → Phase 9:** the fair same-budget **BP+replay *accuracy*** baseline (the existential test; P8 settled *energy*, not accuracy), natural multi-class **A5**, the read-side noise residual, and re-tuning the (drift-rate-conditional) cadence if **N2** slows the drift. Behavioral simulation only; no SPICE. → [`../src/phase8/README.md`](../src/phase8/README.md)

## The drift fix (resolved)

GD breaks if SCFF drifts faster than GD can re-track. This is **not** a contested-weight problem — taps mean SCFF and GD never share a weight. So we bound drift cheaply with the **plasticity gradient** (N2). The budget rule: lower the read-layer learning rate (free) rather than fire GD more often (burns the 80/20). Permutation / re-clustering events are the one thing slow-LR only *delays* → guard with BCM homeostasis (on the existing momentum register) + the multi-layer tap as redundancy.

## Open knobs — the sims decide, not us

- Front:back plasticity ratio — exp 2.1 (frozen vs slow vs fast read-layers).
- Gate threshold — absolute vs plateau-slope.
- Sleep cadence — couples to cluster-churn rate, and to EMA τ if the EMA-view is used.
- How little history sleep needs (3.x); LUT vigilance threshold.
- Margin loss vs log-loss; two-sided vs pure-contrast objective.
- How many chained blocks before direction-chaining strains.
- Tied-from-start vs converge-to-tied first layer — does forcing summation cost any training ease?
