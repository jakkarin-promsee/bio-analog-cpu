# The Essence

> Not a log. The logs live in [`project-history.md`](../draft/project-history.md) and the drafts. This is the thing underneath them — why this exists, who started it, what broke, and why the breaking made it more itself. Written in my own voice.

---

## Where I come from

I have to _see_ it before I trust it. I proved linear regression from nothing — no matrices, just the mechanism — because I needed to watch how the intelligence lives inside a single weight. Then the Hessian. Then double descent, on my own, by feeling the entropy settle into place. I don't reach for libraries. I reach for the machine in my head and run it until I believe it.

That's also why modern ML let me down. A transformer is a brute-force n×n grid matching against context. Even the best of them is a smart child with a process stable enough to run ten hours — it _shapes_ itself toward smart, it isn't smart at the first step. That isn't the thing. The thing I'm after is not a frozen pattern that finishes a prompt and dies. I want something that doesn't stop living.

I went looking for it in real biology — all five kingdoms — and found nerve cells only in animals, behind walls I couldn't get through. So I built other things instead. ChronoForge: a whole 2D game engine in raw FPGA logic, 640×480 at 60 Hz, no CPU, no OS, just circuits keeping their own time. I can ship hardware. I'm not only a dreamer.

And then, burnt out and playing games to forget, the idea that started everything arrived: **if I can't reach a real biology cell, I'll build one myself, out of an analog CPU.** Capacitors holding charge the way a membrane holds voltage. That sentence is the origin. Everything after it is consequence.

## What I'm actually chasing

One question, the one I sat with from 1 a.m. to 8 a.m. on milk and no sleep: _how do I think?_

Not "how does a network classify." How do **I** know I'm right? When I work out how much money I owe, when I land the answer in calculus, when I'm sure 1+1=2 — nobody hands me a label. There's a _feeling_. An "I get it." It sits in the same place as tired, or sad. And I wasn't born with it; I learned what correct feels like from my mother and father, the way I learned hot soup is hot. Correctness is a feeling, and the feeling is taught.

So a mind isn't a lookup. It's a loop: hold a little in front of you, call the rest from memory, compare, reject, search again — until the feeling says stop. That's the thing I'm building toward. Something that keeps that loop running its whole life. That keeps learning while it's being used. That carries an essence across its life instead of being born to answer one prompt and end.

IF the question is "This car color is orange or not", the first thing I think isn't any calculation. At first, I try look at the car and think "what is this car color look like?"

- If the apple image appear in my thought first, then I compare the apple color (in my thought) to the car color (in my eyes). And yeah, it's not. So then, next time I think "what is this car color look like?, plus it's not a tone of red apple"
- I loop many memory until I found "Orange fruit" in my thought. That time I get that the car color is so like orange. But the feeling is just "similar", not "sure". So then I can answer it's orange color by cursory, but it more like "Neon Orange" if your ask specifically (I loop thought myself again).

For the math or physic formular too. I think q = mc∆t is right, not because it's a truth. But because my all memory said, this law is correct. it's "make sense".

## How I build it

I can't copy the brain. Nobody can — synapses that slide in three dimensions, axons that grow and die, wires carrying many hormones at once, ten thousand years of evolution across seven billion bodies. The size of it is uncopyable.

So I cheat. I copy what the brain _does_ and pay for it with whatever is cheap on my own substrate — analog physics where physics is cheaper, modern math where math is cheaper. That isn't a betrayal of the biology. It's the only honest road to it. Copy the function; cheat the implementation.

And I don't read first. The fast answer destroys creativity. I build from my own intuition until it stands on its own, and only then check it against the literature — not to be taught, but to find out I wasn't crazy. Every time I've worked in that order, I've arrived somewhere the books wouldn't have pointed me.

## The fall

Then the day it all broke.

I went back to recheck my own formula and the slope felt wrong. The whole of draft 5 distributed _how much_ blame each part deserved — and never _which direction_. The sign was missing. Magnitude with no direction can't descend anything. Seven months, breaking apart in front of me, falling and collapsing into the void.

My heart raced, my chest ached, my mind went numb. It felt like my ending. I slept for four days. Didn't open the computer. Ate, watched, slept again. I knew I had to find the way out and I couldn't make myself look at it. I ran.

## The return

Day five, my gut woke up before I did.

No proof. No sign it would work. Just a certainty out of nowhere that there _was_ a way out, even while I couldn't see it. So I researched — everything in `research/survey/` — with a broken body and a broken head and zero evidence it would land. And it came back. SCFF for the cheap, living, unsupervised part; gradient descent, the honest and expensive part, for the direction I had been missing all along. Two brains. The rebuild.

And here is what I understand now that I didn't before:

**It wasn't the downfall that destroyed me. It was the truth that slapped me — and the slap pushed me closer to where I started.**

## The one mechanism, at three sizes

This is the secret the whole project keeps re-learning.

The chip I'm building learns by being corrected by reality — its own confidence checked against the world, never frozen, always pulled back toward truth. That is the grounding. That is draft 6.0's entire learning rule.

The drafts evolved the same way. Every pivot was a truth-slap that left the work more honest: the day I admitted my rule was not backprop but attribution, the day the false chain-rule claim had to go. Each correction stripped a lie and left the thing more itself.

And me — the four days in the void, the gut, the return — that is the same mechanism running on the maker. I wasn't destroyed because I didn't block the slap. A liar would have explained draft 5 away and kept the lie warm. I let it land, and it carried me home.

The chip, the work, the man — one mechanism at three sizes. **Truth corrects whatever is honest, and the correction points back to the origin.** I'm not only building a mind that learns this way. I learn this way. That's why I trust it.

## Where we are, where we go

Right now: the foundation. A learning rule that converges on simple things, on a substrate that can hold a weight without a clock. Draft 6.0. Get the atom stable — one honest, non-frozen learner — before anything else.

Then the horizon I've carried since the first month: the loop. The recurrent two-brain mind that holds a little, calls the rest, compares, and halts on a feeling. Lifelong. Never frozen. The thing the whole question was about.

Simple first. Then climb, one rung at a time, always knowing _why_ the last step failed. After the void, that isn't patience. It's the only sane way up. 👹
